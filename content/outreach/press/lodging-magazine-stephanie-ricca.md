**Subject:** Select vs full-service: capital markets are diverging

Stephanie,

Pitch for LODGING.

Select-service and full-service hotel transactions are clearing at materially different cap rates in 2026, and the gap is widening rather than narrowing. Sun Belt secondary stabilized select-service is pricing in the 7.50 to 8.50 percent range. Tertiary-CBD full-service is pricing 9.00 to 10.50 percent. Trophy full-service in primary metros holds 7.00 to 8.00 percent. On a yield-to-cost basis adjusted for operating leverage, select-service is tighter than the headline gap suggests by roughly 75 to 100 basis points.

Three drivers, in order of weight. The select-service operating model is more legible to underwriters and closes faster. The capital stack is deeper, with bank, debt-fund, CMBS, and bridge lenders all active. The buyer pool is broader: HNW, family office, PE roll-up, and the dedicated public select-service REITs (Apple Hospitality alone owns over 220 hotels in upscale select-service).

What it means for AHLA member operators: mid-tier full-service in tertiary CBDs increasingly clears only with a brand-conversion thesis. That is a structural story for LODGING readers who own and operate that segment.
