**Subject:** Guest pitch: when operators should sell, when to refinance

Steve, David,

Pitching Luke Thompson, our capital markets director at Matthews Hotel Markets, as a guest. Modern Hotelier's audience leans operator and tech-forward; the angle that fits is the disposition decision from the operator chair.

Episode angle: the sell vs refinance vs hold decision in 2026, framed for operators.

Most operators we talk with are running a refinance pro forma in isolation when their CMBS debt is coming up to maturity. The cap rate band has compressed enough off the 2024 peak that selling at current pricing frequently produces equity recovery comparable to refinancing with a fresh equity check, with the upside of redeploying into a new vintage. Luke can walk through the side-by-side: what the math looks like, where the proceeds gap clusters, how to read the buyer pool by segment, and the operating moves (F&B repositioning, group-pace cleanup, brand-conversion underwriting) that change the disposition outcome before you go to market.

Concrete data points he can bring: Sun Belt secondary stabilized select-service is pricing 7.50 to 8.50 percent. Tertiary-CBD full-service is pricing 9.00 to 10.50 percent. Lodging is 20.5 percent of the $76.6B 2026 CMBS hard-maturity book per Trepp. The Walden Retreats Hill Country close is a fresh data point on Sun Belt lifestyle bid depth.

Luke is in Austin, has done remote long-form, can flex to your recording cadence.
