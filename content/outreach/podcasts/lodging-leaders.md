**Subject:** Guest pitch: select-service is the bid in 2026

Jonathan, Judy,

Pitching Luke Thompson, capital markets director at Matthews Hotel Markets, as a Lodging Leaders guest.

Episode angle: why select-service is the most actively bid hospitality category in 2026, and what full-service has to do to compete.

The capital markets divergence is real and structural. Select-service in Sun Belt secondary markets is pricing 7.50 to 8.50 percent. Tertiary-CBD full-service is pricing 9.00 to 10.50 percent. On a yield-to-cost basis adjusted for operating leverage the gap is wider than the headline numbers show. Three drivers: the operating model is more legible to underwriters, the capital stack is deeper, the buyer pool is broader (Apple Hospitality alone owns over 220 hotels, and the family-office and PE bid is now active at current pricing).

What Luke can offer your asset-management and ownership audience:

- The cap rate band by segment and sub-market, with named buyer types.
- The brand-conversion underwriting case for mid-tier full-service in tertiary CBDs (often the highest-value path for that segment).
- Operating-side moves that recover 50 to 75 basis points of cap rate at sale: F&B repositioning before going to market, segment-rationalizing the asset list, narrative sequencing.
- Fresh perspective from the Walden Retreats Hill Country close: a lifestyle resort that drew a deeper institutional bid than the 2024 narrative would have predicted.

Luke is in Austin, can do remote.
