**Subject:** Cornell CHR data partnership proposal

CHR research team,

Brief proposal for a data partnership between Matthews Hotel Markets and the Center for Hospitality Research.

What we are offering. An anonymized Matthews Hotel Markets transaction file covering U.S. hotel sales we have brokered or co-brokered, with cap rate, segment, brand, sub-market, key count, and stabilized NOI fields. Initial file covers the 2023 to 2026 cycle. Refresh quarterly thereafter. Anonymization protocol: property identifiers stripped, sub-market aggregated to a level that prevents asset re-identification, individual seller and buyer identifiers stripped or hashed. The file is suitable for academic working-paper use under a standard data-use agreement.

What we are asking from CHR. A co-authored or CHR-led working paper using the data, addressed to one or more of: the select-service vs full-service capital markets divergence in 2026, Sun Belt secondary cap rate compression mechanics, the 2026 to 2027 hotel CMBS maturity wall and refinance proceeds gap, or brand-flag pricing dynamics. Matthews is happy to be cited as the data source or to share a contributor byline depending on what fits CHR editorial standards. We are not asking for marketing language inside the paper; we are asking for academic treatment of a data set that is otherwise hard for outside researchers to access.

Why this works for CHR. The data has not been previously released to academic research. It captures the broker-level cap rate detail that public sources (HVS, STR, AHLA aggregates, public REIT filings) do not. It is refreshed at a cadence that supports a recurring CHR briefing series.

Why this works for Matthews. Distribution through CHR's research channels and the Cornell hospitality alumni network. Academic legitimacy on data that we currently use only in client-facing memos.

Glad to schedule a 30-minute call this month or to send a sample anonymized data extract on request. Luke Thompson and Nate Solomon are the named brokers; either of us can be the Matthews-side principal investigator on the project.
