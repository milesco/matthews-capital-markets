## Draft 1 — Nate Solomon (Hospitality Associate)

Texas added 563,000 net new residents in the year ending July 2024. Florida added 467,000.

That is the largest two-state population gain in the U.S., per the Census Bureau's Vintage 2024 estimates released December 2024. North Carolina, Georgia, Tennessee, South Carolina, and Arizona each added between 90,000 and 200,000. Net Sun Belt domestic migration far exceeded the combined net outflow from California, New York, Illinois, and New Jersey.

The contrarian read: the institutional consensus still anchors Sun Belt allocation in primary metros (Atlanta, Nashville, Austin) for liquidity and exit-comp reasons. The fastest-moving capital today is family-office, and family-office is buying Sun Belt secondary (Knoxville, Tyler, Lubbock, College Station, Mobile, Greenville). The cap-rate spread is 75 to 125 basis points wide. The supply pipeline is constrained through 2027. The math works in the secondary basket in a way most institutional underwriters have not yet caught up to.

Three out of four hotel investors who deployed into primary urban metros in the 2018 to 2022 vintage are now actively rotating Sun Belt secondary on the next vintage in conversations I am part of.

Which Sun Belt secondary market are you tracking most closely right now?

## Draft 2 — Luke Thompson (VP & Director, Capital Markets)

The Sun Belt hotel story everyone is telling in 2026 is the population growth story. The story I think is more under-priced is the supply story.

STR's Q1 2026 U.S. hotel construction pipeline report, released April 2026, shows total U.S. rooms under construction running below the 2019 baseline. The 2022 to 2024 construction-loan freeze interrupted hotel ground-breakings across the Sun Belt. The thaw began mid-2025 for sponsors with track records, but the gap through 2027 is structural. Forward RevPAR is supported not just by demand growth but by the absence of new supply.

The contrarian piece: supply discipline is doing more work in Sun Belt secondary markets right now than population growth or migration. Population converts to lodging demand on an 18- to 24-month lag. Supply pipelines convert to RevPAR on day one. The markets where the construction-loan freeze interrupted the most ground-breakings are the markets where forward ADR has the cleanest path higher.

AHLA projects 2026 U.S. demand at 1.349 billion sold room nights — a record. A disproportionate share of that incremental demand will land where the new supply did not arrive.

Which Sun Belt market do you think is most under-supplied through 2027?
