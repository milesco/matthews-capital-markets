## Draft 1 — Luke Thompson

Hotel disposition timelines tightened in 2026, and most sellers I talk to are still pricing in 2024 conditions.

Median engagement-letter-to-closing-wire on a clean single-asset select-service deal now runs 22 to 28 weeks (PwC Hospitality Directions US, December 2025). Sun Belt secondary stabilized PIP-current product is producing 6 to 14 first-round bids in our 2026 file, versus 3 to 7 in 2024. Closing timelines have compressed roughly 2 to 4 weeks as franchisor approvals stabilized and buyer diligence teams rebuilt capacity.

The two decisions that matter happen before marketing launches: pre-listing PIP strategy and debt assumption versus payoff. A property facing a $1.5M PIP at a 7.75 percent post-renovation cap rate carries roughly $2.25 to $3M of buyer-applied valuation drag. Assumable debt at coupons more than 100 bps below market compresses cap rates 25 to 75 bps; less than 50 bps spread typically supports payoff.

Contrarian view: the highest-leverage decision in most disposition processes is not the asking price. It is the call on PIP and debt strategy in week one. Both decisions get made before the broker writes the OM.

If you have a 2026 or 2027 disposition window in mind, what is the math telling you on PIP and debt?

## Draft 2 — Nate Solomon

Most hotel sellers I work with want to know two things on the first call: how long will this take, and what will it cost.

In 2026: 22 to 28 weeks from engagement letter to closing wire on a clean single-asset select-service hotel (PwC Hospitality Directions US, December 2025). Industry-typical broker fees slide by deal size. Under $10M: 2.0 to 2.5 percent. $10M to $30M: 1.5 to 2.0 percent. $30M to $75M: 1.25 to 1.75 percent. Above $75M: 1.0 to 1.5 percent. Portfolios above $150M: 0.75 to 1.25 percent.

Three franchisor approvals (Hilton, Marriott, IHG) each take 60 to 120 days from a complete application. That timeline runs in parallel with PSA negotiation and due diligence, not after.

Contrarian take: there are real cases in 2026 where the right answer is not to sell. If you are mid-PIP-cycle without the capital to complete pre-sale, or you have in-place debt at a coupon meaningfully below market that is not assumable, the math often supports refinancing and holding through the next cycle. We run BOVs alongside refinance evaluations for clients facing this question so the decision is on paper, not in a feeling.
