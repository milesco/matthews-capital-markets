## Draft 1 — Luke Thompson (VP & Director, Capital Markets)

Roughly $30 billion of U.S. hotel CMBS matures through year-end 2027.

Hotel CMBS delinquency closed Q1 2026 at 7.40 percent, the highest property-type rate in the index (Trepp, April 2026 monthly report). Most of that paper was originated when the 10-year Treasury sat at 2.50 to 3.00 percent. It closed last Friday at 4.36 percent.

The contrarian read: this is not a distress wave for stabilized PIP-current select-service in Sun Belt secondary markets. The owners holding that paper are largely owner-operators with long hold horizons and clean balance sheets. The distress sits in mid-tier full-service in tertiary CBDs, not in the Hampton Inns and Holiday Inn Express boxes most of the maturity stack actually represents.

The honest framework runs through four paths: bridge-to-perm, A/B note restructure, recap with fresh preferred equity, or sale at the new cap rate. We are running BOVs alongside refinance scenarios for clients facing 2026 and 2027 maturities. The cost of running both paths is small. The decision-making clarity is large.

## Draft 2 — Nate Solomon (Hospitality Associate)

I keep hearing "refinancing wave" and "distress cycle" used interchangeably in 2026 hotel conversations. They are not the same thing.

Trepp's March 2026 surveillance puts roughly $30 billion of U.S. hotel CMBS coming due through year-end 2027. The Mortgage Bankers Association reported $957 billion in total commercial and multifamily maturities scheduled for 2025 alone. Yes, that is a wave. No, it is not all distress.

Here is the contrarian piece: the proceeds-to-payoff gap on stabilized PIP-current select-service in Sun Belt secondary markets is real, but the bid for those assets is also tighter than it has been since 2022. For a meaningful share of owners, selling at the new cap rate produces a better outcome than writing a fresh equity check. The arithmetic works.

Owners I am working with are increasingly running the BOV in parallel with the refinance scenario. The path you choose depends on your forward RevPAR view, your equity availability, your PIP timing, and the asset's strategic role in your portfolio. Not on the headline number for hotel CMBS distress.
