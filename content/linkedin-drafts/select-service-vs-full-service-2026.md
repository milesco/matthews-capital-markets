## Draft 1 — Luke Thompson

The cap rate gap between select-service and full-service is widening in 2026, not narrowing.

Sun Belt secondary stabilized select-service prices 7.50 to 8.50 percent. Tertiary-CBD full-service prices 9.00 to 10.50 percent. That is a 150 to 200 basis-point spread on the headline number, and on a yield-to-cost basis the gap is wider, not smaller.

The contrarian read for full-service owners: the brand flag is not the asset. Some full-service properties are full-service only because the brand standards require it. They function as upscale select-service economically. Sponsors who position the disposition narrative around the operating reality (compact F&B, lean group sales, premium ADR) instead of the brand requirement frequently recover 50 to 75 basis points of cap rate at sale.

The other contrarian read: brand conversion is not a last resort. The most efficient use of capital for sponsors holding mid-tier full-service in tertiary CBDs is often to underwrite a conversion to a select-service flag and price the asset accordingly. The post-conversion stabilized exit prices 100 to 200 basis points tighter than the pre-conversion full-service band.

Roughly $30 billion in U.S. hotel CMBS matures through year-end 2027. The proceeds gap is wider for full-service. Owners with 2026 or 2027 maturities need a framework now.

## Draft 2 — Nate Solomon

Apple Hospitality REIT owns 220-plus hotels and almost all of them are upscale select-service.

That fact alone explains why the cap rate gap between select-service and full-service is structural and not cyclical.

The buyer pool for select-service is broader than people realize. HNW first-time hotel buyers, family offices, PE roll-ups, and three dedicated public REITs (Apple, Summit, Chatham) all underwrite the segment. The buyer pool for full-service in tertiary CBDs is much narrower; the deep institutional bid concentrates on trophy assets in primary metros, and below that tier the menu of capable buyers gets short fast.

The contrarian implication: select-service is not the safer asset class because the operating model is simpler. It is the more-bid asset class because the bid is structurally deeper. Those are different reasons and they have different durations.

For owner-operators evaluating their next acquisition: the segment with the broadest buyer pool today is also the segment with the cleanest forward supply pipeline through 2027. That is not a coincidence.
