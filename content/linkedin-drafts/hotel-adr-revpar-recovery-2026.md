# LinkedIn Drafts — Hotel ADR + RevPAR Recovery 2026

## Draft 1 — Luke Thompson

STR/CoStar's first 2026 forecast: full-year U.S. RevPAR growth of 0.6 percent, ADR up 1.0 percent, occupancy at 62.1 percent.

The 2026 World Cup contributes 0.4 percent of that 0.6 percent. Strip the tournament out and organic RevPAR growth is roughly 0.2 percent.

That is a flat headline. Underneath sits the widest market-by-market dispersion of the cycle.

The bifurcation is real. Luxury and upper-upscale are still posting RevPAR gains. Select-service and economy are flat to slightly negative. Supply-constrained leisure markets like Charleston, Miami, Scottsdale resort, and Orlando are forecasting +2.5 to +5.0 percent. Suburban Austin and suburban Nashville are absorbing 2024-2025 deliveries at the expense of pricing.

Contrarian read: the consensus narrative says select-service is the safe bid for 2026. The data says select-service in the metros where construction was concentrated through 2024-2025 will absorb supply at the expense of RevPAR. Underwrite those markets to 70 to 80 percent of stabilized for year one. The safer play is supply-constrained leisure where new construction is structurally difficult.

Lodging Econometrics: 2026 supply growth at 1.4 percent, total pipeline down 5 percent YoY by project count. The construction-loan freeze of 2022 to early 2025 left a durable supply-side gap that begins to compound through 2027. That is the bid for 2027 and 2028, not 2026.

## Draft 2 — Nate Solomon

The 2025 hotel print is the data point that should change every 2026 underwriting model: U.S. RevPAR fell 0.3 percent, the first non-recessionary RevPAR decline ever recorded in the American hotel industry.

That is not a cyclical print. That is a structural inflection.

What CoStar tells us about 2026: 0.6 percent RevPAR growth, 1.0 percent ADR, occupancy at 62.1 percent. The 2026 World Cup adds 0.4 percent of that on its own. The underlying organic forecast is essentially flat.

Three places the data says will outperform the consensus.

One: Charleston. Historic-district full-service ADR of $300-450 at peak, structural supply constraint, Boeing/Mercedes industrial demand layer. Base case +2.5 to +4.0 percent RevPAR.

Two: Orlando. Universal Epic Universe (opened 2025) materially shifted demand patterns. Industry estimates put 2026 RevPAR near $147.83 with occupancy at 72.0 percent. Base case +2.5 to +4.0 percent RevPAR.

Three: Scottsdale resort. March 2026 RevPAR hit $339, up from $279 a year earlier. Spring training, WM Phoenix Open, Barrett-Jackson all drive Q1 compression. Base case +3.0 to +5.0 percent RevPAR.

Three places the data says to discount the consensus. Suburban Austin (2024-2025 supply absorbing). Suburban Nashville (similar). Tampa (January 2026 occupancy fell 14.9 percent YoY — recovery is the variable).
