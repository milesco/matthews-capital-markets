## Draft 1 — Luke Thompson

Texas hotel cap rates compressed 25 to 50 basis points off the 2024 peak through the first half of 2026.

That is not a rate-cut trade. It is the bid pool rebuilding around stabilized select-service because the underwriting math now closes at current debt cost.

Where the band sits in Q2 2026:

Austin select-service: 7.50 to 8.25 percent. Hill Country resort: 6.50 to 7.75 percent.
DFW select-service: 7.75 to 8.50 percent. Convention-adjacent full-service: 7.00 to 7.75 percent.
Houston select-service: 7.75 to 8.75 percent, the widest of the four metros, on energy-cycle exposure. Medical Center proximity trades roughly 50 bps tighter.
San Antonio Riverwalk full-service: 6.75 to 7.50 percent, the tightest urban band in the state.

The contrarian read: family-office and developer-sponsor capital is the bid-side story this quarter, not institutional. Funds are still recalibrating. The capital that accepted the new mid-band first is winning the deals.

If you have a 2026 or 2027 maturity on a Texas hotel, run the refinance and disposition paths in parallel before the calendar dictates the answer for you.

## Draft 2 — Nate Solomon

Houston cap rates are the widest in Texas at 7.75 to 8.75 percent on stabilized select-service.

Most underwriting memos I read still treat that as a permanent discount. It is not.

Houston metro RevPAR ran roughly 4 percent above the 2019 baseline through Q1 2026. The energy-cycle premium the institutional bid prices in does not show up in the actual demand data the way it used to. The Texas Medical Center sub-market alone clears 50 basis points tighter than the metro average and never re-prices to the metro band on a sale.

The opportunity is sub-market specific. A medical-adjacent Hampton Inn or a Woodlands corporate-driven Holiday Inn Express does not deserve to underwrite at the same cap rate as an Energy Corridor box exposed to one tenant cycle.

What I am watching in Q3: whether the family-office capital that has been the most active Houston buyer this year keeps its pace, or whether PE roll-ups close the gap.
