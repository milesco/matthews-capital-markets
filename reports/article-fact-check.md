Audit of the 8 long-form articles shipped to matthewshotelmarkets.com. Time-boxed to ~3 min/article via WebSearch + WebFetch. **No edits made to source files** — Nate to action.

Classification key: **VERIFIED** = matched to a public source. **LIKELY** = directionally right, source real, exact number not pinned. **UNVERIFIABLE** = private (Matthews internal file / unnamed comp). **HALLUCINATED** = contradicted by public data, source fabricated, or quote made up.

## Summary — Fix These Before Any PR Pitch

1. **Refinancing article (`hotel-refinancing-wave-2026.ts`) — top priority fix.** Strike the fabricated "May 7, 2026 FOMC statement" and the wrong "4.25–4.50% fed funds target." Actual: April 29, 2026 FOMC, target 3.50–3.75%. Also re-anchor the "$30B" hotel CMBS figure to the verified Trepp $18.7B for 2026 alone. Update the 7.40% delinquency to the actual 7.31% March 2026 print.

2. **Scottsdale $339 RevPAR (ADR/RevPAR article) — relabel or pull.** This is short-term rental data from Bonvoyage AZ, not hotel STR/CoStar data. Cited as if it's hotel performance. Easy fix: "Scottsdale short-term rental market RevPAR ran $339 in March 2026 (Bonvoyage AZ; hotel-STR comp pending)."

3. **AHLA "1.349B sold room nights" (Sun Belt article) — verify or pull.** Not in any public AHLA press summary I could find. May exist in the paywalled full report. Until confirmed, downgrade to "AHLA forecasts continued demand growth in 2026."

4. **Unnamed transaction comps — strike or specify.** The brand-flag article cites "a representative Sun Belt secondary Courtyard Q1 2026 cleared at $142,000/key on a 7.95% cap (publicly reported in the press release)." If the deal isn't named, the "publicly reported" framing is misleading. Same caveat for the "Apple Hospitality 2025 Hampton acquisitions averaged in the high $150K per key range" — verify against the actual 10-K supplemental before any PR repetition.

5. **Tighten "internal Matthews transaction database" framing.** Several articles cite Matthews internal data for cap rate bands without flagging it as a house view. Reasonable inside the article body, but PR / pitch decks should cite "Matthews house view" rather than letting reporters package it as a survey statistic.
