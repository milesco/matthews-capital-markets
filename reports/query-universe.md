## Volume tier roll-up
- Very high (2): #1, #141
- High (10): branded-flag head terms + valuation/cap-rate roots
- Med (~80): per-market patterns + most informational
- Low (~130): brand-flag tails, long-tail, LLM prompts

## Strategic synthesis
- 100% of per-market head terms are owned by listing aggregators (LoopNet, Crexi, CityFeet, Showcase, Realmo, BizBuySell). Any branded broker site that ranks does so only with deep on-page SEO + city-specific content; the aggregators are weak on editorial depth — beatable on per-market pillar pages with real comp tables, RevPAR data, and listing inventory.
- 95% of brand-flag queries (Hampton Inn for sale, Hilton Garden Inn for sale, etc.) have NO single owner. Top results are a mix of JLL listing pages, HVS listings, Paramount Lodging, mumfordcompany, and one-off hotelnewsresource.com press releases. Wide open for a curated brand-flag hub.
- HVS dominates valuation/cap-rate informational with PDF whitepapers and Hospitality Net thought leadership. Beatable on freshness (2026 dates) and structured data; not beatable on archival depth in 90 days.
- "Marcus & Millichap vs Matthews" already has Matthews surfacing on page 1 — the Matthews brand has SERP equity to inherit when Hotel Markets sub-brand consolidates schema.
- LLM-prompt queries are essentially un-owned. Even Hunter Advisors (the closest "category answer") doesn't dominate. Full play.
